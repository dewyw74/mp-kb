# Akai MPK Mini Plus 37-Key Controller Profile

The Akai MPK Mini Plus 37 is best treated as a compact all-purpose writing controller: 37 keys for chords, basslines, and hooks; pads for Drum Rack or sample triggering; knobs for macro control; and transport-style controls for session navigation where supported.

## Best roles

- Ableton Drum Rack pad triggering.
- Bassline and chord sketching.
- Two-handed simple voicings that are awkward on smaller 25-key controllers.
- Macro control for Instrument Racks and Audio Effect Racks.
- Filter, resonance, reverb, delay, width, and saturation performance.
- Compact travel or desktop production setup.

## Ableton setup

Enable the MPK Mini Plus 37 input in Ableton's MIDI preferences:

- Track: on, for playing instruments and Drum Racks.
- Remote: on, for MIDI mapping knobs and buttons.
- Sync: usually off unless a hardware/sequencer feature specifically needs clock.

Use Ableton's MIDI Map Mode for project-level controls, but prefer rack macros for reusable mappings.

## Suggested knob map

Use the same eight macro layout across racks:

- Knob 1: Brightness
- Knob 2: Movement
- Knob 3: Grit
- Knob 4: Width
- Knob 5: Pump
- Knob 6: Space
- Knob 7: Delay
- Knob 8: Level or Energy

This gives the controller a stable muscle-memory layout across bass, lead, pad, drum, and FX racks.

## 37-key writing advantage

Compared with 25-key controllers, the 37-key range makes the MPK Mini Plus more useful for actual writing rather than only note entry. It can handle:

- Left-hand root notes with right-hand triads.
- Bassline sketching without constant octave switching.
- Chord-stab voicings for house, future bass, synth-pop, and rock demos.
- Lead hooks with enough range for pitch movement and octave variation.

For full piano parts it is still compact, but for production writing it is enough range to keep ideas moving quickly.

## Pad workflow

Use pads for Drum Rack first:

- Kick, snare or clap, closed hat, open hat.
- Percussion, fill, impact, vocal chop.
- Scene or clip launching only if the set is designed around performance.

For finger drumming, record unquantized, then use partial quantization and velocity cleanup rather than flattening every hit to the grid.

## Common mistakes

Do not map knobs differently in every project. The controller becomes much more useful when each knob has a consistent musical purpose.

Do not use the pads only as clip launch buttons if the session needs drum feel. The MPK Mini Plus 37 is especially useful when the pads actually perform rhythms into Drum Rack while the keys handle bass, chords, or hooks.
