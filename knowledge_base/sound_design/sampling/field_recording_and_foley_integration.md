# Field Recording and Foley Integration into Music Production

Field recording (capturing real-world environmental or object sound outside a studio) and foley (recording deliberate, often performed physical sounds — impacts, footsteps, scrapes — for later use) both function across this knowledge base as legitimate primary sound sources, not just occasional atmosphere dressing. Genre files consulted for grounding: `knowledge_base/genres/edm/industrial_techno.md`, `minimal_techno.md`, `melodic_techno.md`, `psybient.md`, `knowledge_base/genres/electronic/dark_ambient.md`, `ambient.md`, `deconstructed_club.md`, `knowledge_base/genres/metal/atmospheric_black_metal.md`, `knowledge_base/genres/rock/post_rock.md`, `knowledge_base/genres/classical/neoclassical.md`, `knowledge_base/genres/cinematic/horror_score.md`.

## Recorded Object and Environment Sources as Percussion

Several genres build actual drum/percussion elements directly from recorded real-world sound rather than sampled or synthesized drum hits:

- `industrial_techno.md` documents this as a defining technique, not a texture layer: "metallic, clanging percussion samples (industrial found-sound/foley-style hits)" combined with the kick, and explicitly names the capture method — "Field-recording and foley-sampling real metal/machinery sounds, then heavily processing (pitching, distorting, granulating) them into percussion and texture elements" — with the file recommending producers "source or design metallic, mechanical, foley-style percussion samples (factory noise, metal impacts) rather than relying purely on conventional drum-machine sounds."
- `minimal_techno.md` credits this specifically to Robert Hood's production practice: "Field-recording/foley-based sound design for unique, non-musical percussive textures (a Robert Hood/minimal-techno signature technique)," recommending producers "field-record and foley-process everyday sounds into percussive elements for a distinctive, non-derivative sonic palette."
- `deconstructed_club.md` documents an even more extreme, deliberately non-musical version: "found sound, foley-style samples (breaking glass, impacts, vocal exclamations)... function as texture" in place of conventional orchestration, with "foley/sample layering (glass, impacts, mechanical sounds) as a percussive sound-design technique" layered alongside programmed drums for an aggressive, non-musical percussive character.

## Field Recordings as Atmosphere and Drone Material

A separate, larger family of genre files uses field recordings not as rhythmic material but as sustained textural/atmospheric beds, often processed heavily:

- `dark_ambient.md` treats real-world recordings as the genre's primary raw material rather than an accent: "Processed field recordings (industrial machinery, caves, weather)" sit alongside horror-score-style string clusters and ritualistic percussion samples, with production notes explicit about *why*: "Source raw material from field recordings of resonant, unsettling real-world spaces (industrial machinery, caves, metal structures) and process heavily — real-world grain reads as more unsettling than pure synthesis." The file also documents granular resynthesis of field recordings for "organic, unrepeating textures."
- `ambient.md` uses field recording as a structural device as much as a textural one: tracks frequently "begin from silence or near-silence; a single pad, drone, or field recording fades in over 30-90 seconds with no discrete downbeat," and a bridge section is often marked by "the introduction of a foreign timbre (voice, field recording, detuned bell)."
- `atmospheric_black_metal.md` documents field recordings (specifically nature sounds — wind, rain, forest ambience) as a genre-signature device reinforcing thematic content, "pioneered by Wolves in the Throne Room," frequently opening tracks and layered beneath tremolo-picked guitar passages.
- `post_rock.md` names Godspeed You! Black Emperor's use of "field recordings, spoken-word samples, and found sound... layered into intros/outros for atmosphere and narrative texture" as a signature technique, often the literal opening sound of a piece before any instrument enters.
- `psybient.md` and `melodic_techno.md` both use field recordings as raw material for granular processing into evolving texture: `psybient.md` documents "extensive time-stretching of samples (world instruments, field recordings) to remove their original rhythmic identity and repurpose them as pure texture," and `melodic_techno.md` documents "processed vocal samples, field recordings blended with synths" for hybrid organic-electronic cinematic atmosphere.
- `neoclassical.md` uses field recordings for a warmer, more intimate purpose than the ambient/dark-ambient examples above: "layering field recordings and subtle tape-style processing for a warm, intimate, slightly worn sonic signature" alongside solo piano and small string ensemble.
- `horror_score.md` combines field recordings with content below the range of normal hearing for physical unease: "layer processed field recordings and infrasound (very low, near-inaudible frequency content) with orchestral drones for a physically unsettling low-end presence."

## Capture and Processing Workflow

The common workflow across these genre files has three stages, whether the end goal is percussion or atmosphere:

1. **Capture** — a field recorder (or a close-mic'd object recording in a controlled setting, the foley side of the technique) captures the raw source: machinery, metal impacts, weather, room tone, or a performed physical action.
2. **Isolate and edit** — the useful portion of the recording is trimmed and cleaned of unwanted noise, exactly as with any other sample source (see `chopping_flipping_and_time_stretching.md` for the general editing technique).
3. **Process toward the target role** — for percussion, this typically means pitching the hit to a usable drum register, transient-shaping it for punch, and layering it against (or replacing) a conventional drum sound, per `industrial_techno.md`'s and `minimal_techno.md`'s workflow. For atmosphere, this typically means heavy time-stretching, granular resynthesis, reverb, or drone-layering per `dark_ambient.md` and `psybient.md` — the goal there is usually to dissolve the recording's specific identity into pure texture rather than keep it recognizable.

## Common Mistakes

**Leaving a raw field recording unprocessed and expecting it to sit in a mix like a designed sound.** Every genre file above pairs "capture real-world sound" with a deliberate processing stage (pitching, granulating, distorting, time-stretching) — the raw capture is a starting point, not a finished element.

**Treating field-recorded percussion as a novelty rather than a legitimate substitute for a drum-machine hit.** `industrial_techno.md` and `minimal_techno.md` both document foley-derived percussion as a core, structural sound-design choice specific to those genres' identities, not an occasional easter egg.

**Using field recordings for atmosphere without giving them a processing purpose (dissolve into texture vs. stay recognizable as narrative content).** `post_rock.md`'s narrative found-sound intros and `dark_ambient.md`'s dissolved-into-drone processing are different goals requiring different treatment of the same raw material.
