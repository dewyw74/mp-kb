## History

Rage rap's foundations trace to Playboi Carti's 2018 album Die Lit, but the subgenre was decisively defined by his second studio album Whole Lotta Red, released December 25, 2020 through AWGE and Interscope Records — an experimental hip-hop, rage, and trap record incorporating punk and electronic influences that diverged sharply from the melodic trap of his earlier work. The album was primarily produced by F1lthy and Art Dealer, with contributions from Pi'erre Bourne, Maaly Raw, Lil 88, Jasper Harris, Ojivolta, and Wheezy, and executive-produced by Kanye West and Matthew Williams; F1lthy, of the Working on Dying collective, is most responsible for the harsher, more abrasive direction the genre took, building beats from distorted synthesizers, crushing 808s, and a deliberate ugliness on tracks like "Stop Breathing" and "Rockstar Made," while Art Dealer's more ambient, ethereal beats ("ILoveUIHateU," "Over") provided contrast. The genre's name itself derives from "Miss the Rage," a 2021 track by Trippie Redd and Playboi Carti; Whole Lotta Red was subsequently credited with defining the rage subgenre of trap music and influencing artists including Yeat, Ken Carson, and Destroy Lonely, along with much of the current underground rap scene.

## Music Theory

Rage rap's harmonic vocabulary is minimal by design — short, looping, stereo-wide synth motifs and static one-chord drones rather than functional chord progressions — with musical complexity deliberately sacrificed in favor of maximal timbral aggression and rhythmic chaos.

## Arrangement

Tracks open with minimal buildup — a producer tag, synth riser, or distorted 808 hit — establishing chaotic, high-energy intensity from the outset rather than through gradual escalation. Verses favor aggressive, ad-lib-dense, sometimes near-screamed delivery over dense lyrical content, with repetitive, chanted, or screamed hooks designed for mosh-pit-style live performance, and beat switches or abrupt texture changes pushing the genre's structural chaos further than standard trap convention.

## Instruments

The genre's instrumental core is 808 drum machine programming pushed toward greater distortion and density than mainstream trap, paired with short, looping, stereo-wide synth leads built from supersaw or detuned arpeggios drawn directly from EDM and future-bass vocabulary. This maximalist, producer-driven approach was pioneered specifically by F1lthy, Art Dealer, and Pi'erre Bourne on Whole Lotta Red.

## Sound Design

Heavily distorted, bit-crushed 808 bass and supersaw/detuned arpeggiated synth leads define the genre's chaotic sonic signature, with dramatic filter sweeps and risers functioning as core structural devices at section transitions. Screamed, heavily processed vocal ad-libs are layered densely throughout, and deliberately harsh, distorted mix processing embraces digital "ugliness" as an aesthetic rather than a flaw.

## MIDI Programming

Program a heavily distorted 808 bass line and dense, rapid hi-hat rolls pushed toward greater density and distortion than mainstream trap, writing short, looping, stereo-wide synth motifs that function as chaotic textural hooks rather than conventional resolving melodies. Velocity should run high and near-maximal throughout, reflecting the genre's aggressive, high-energy character, with screamed, ad-lib-dense vocal delivery carrying the human, chaotic element.

## Mixing

EQ stays aggressive and bright, emphasizing the cutting presence of distorted synth leads and crushed 808s while deliberately retaining harshness rather than smoothing it out; heavy compression and saturation contribute to the genre's dense, wall-of-sound intensity. Reverb is used selectively for atmospheric contrast in more ambient sections, while automation drives dramatic filter sweeps, riser buildups, and abrupt beat switches central to the genre's chaotic structure.

## Mastering

Masters run aggressively loud at -7 to -5 LUFS integrated with low dynamic range and sustained maximal intensity throughout. Frequency balance favors an aggressive, distorted low end, bright and abrasive synth-lead presence, and a dense, ad-lib-heavy vocal midrange.

## Reference Artists

Playboi Carti's "Stop Breathing" and "Rockstar Made" (both from Whole Lotta Red) define the genre's harsher F1lthy-produced wing, while "ILoveUIHateU" showcases Art Dealer's more atmospheric contribution; "Miss the Rage" (feat. Trippie Redd) gave the genre its name, with Ken Carson and Yeat carrying the sound forward.

## Production Notes

Build the beat around a distorted, crushed 808 and a short, looping, stereo-wide synth motif, layering dense, screamed ad-libs throughout the verse as a primary rhythmic and textural element rather than an occasional accent. Avoid treating rage rap as identical to standard trap or over-cleaning its intentionally harsh, distorted sound design — both undermine the chaotic, maximalist energy that F1lthy's and Art Dealer's Whole Lotta Red production established as the genre's foundation.
