## History

Emo rap originated in the SoundCloud rap scene in the mid-2010s, fusing hip-hop's beats and rapping conventions with the lyrical themes, instrumentals, and vocal delivery commonly found in emo music, particularly 2000s Midwest emo and emo-pop. Lil Peep's music and rapid SoundCloud popularity helped establish the genre as a distinct commercial force, with producers like Nedarb Nagrom and Greaf shaping the sound alongside him; artists including Lil Uzi Vert, Juice WRLD, and XXXTentacion have all cited Lil Peep as a major influence, with Juice WRLD stating directly that "Peep was the first one to really blow up off that sound." The genre reached mainstream commercial prominence in the late 2010s — Lil Peep, XXXTentacion, and Juice WRLD all achieved major, in several cases posthumous, success roughly between 2016 and 2020 — and went on to influence mainstream artists including Post Malone and Future.

## Music Theory

Emo rap harmony draws on simple, melancholic minor-key progressions borrowed directly from emo and pop-punk guitar tradition, layered over static or slow-moving atmospheric vamps inherited from cloud rap production. Guitar-sampled chord loops, frequently drawn from or emulating classic Midwest emo and emo-pop guitar tone, provide the genre's essential melodic backbone rather than functioning as mere textural color.

## Arrangement

Tracks typically open with a sampled or emulated emo/pop-punk guitar figure establishing melancholic atmosphere before verses blend sung and rapped delivery, prioritizing melodic phrasing and emotional directness over dense lyrical content. The hook functions as the genre's clearest melodic and emotional payoff, frequently the most heavily Auto-Tuned and melodically developed section, with tracks often fading back to the opening guitar figure to bookend the emotional arc.

## Instruments

Sampled or performed clean, lightly distorted emo and pop-punk-style guitar is the genre's defining melodic and harmonic voice, paired with subdued, trap-influenced drum programming and understated 808 sub-bass that stay supportive rather than dominant. This sparse production approach — guitar, trap drums, 808 bass, heavily processed vocal — prioritizes mood and melody over dense instrumental arrangement.

## Sound Design

Heavy Auto-Tune on vocals is a defining genre-wide production choice, bridging rap delivery and sung melody to convey emotional vulnerability, layered with extensive reverb on vocals and guitar inherited directly from cloud rap production for an immersive, melancholic atmosphere. Layering multiple vocal takes with Auto-Tune processing produces an emotionally raw yet melodically polished vocal texture central to the genre's identity.

## MIDI Programming

Write mournful, melodic guitar figures in the Midwest emo/emo-pop tradition as the genre's primary melodic backbone, treating vocal melody as equally important content rather than secondary to a rap flow. Natural, emotionally expressive vocal timing is essential to conveying vulnerability and melancholy — a rigidly quantized delivery works against the genre's core emotional aesthetic despite its heavy reliance on Auto-Tune.

## Mixing

EQ should stay warm and melodic, giving clear presence to both guitar and vocal since both function as co-equal melodic carriers, with extensive reverb on vocals and guitar (inherited from cloud rap) providing the genre's signature immersive, melancholic atmosphere. Moderate vocal compression maintains consistent melodic presence within this reverb-heavy, atmospheric mix.

## Mastering

Masters typically target -9 to -7 LUFS integrated for streaming release, balancing loudness competitiveness against the genre's atmospheric, emotionally expressive character, with a warm guitar/vocal midrange balance and soft, non-harsh highs.

## Reference Artists

Lil Peep's "Awful Things" and "Star Shopping" define the genre's foundational guitar-and-vocal melodic template; Juice WRLD's "Lucid Dreams" and XXXTentacion's "Jocelyn Flores" represent the genre's mainstream commercial breakthrough and continued emotional, melodically-driven songwriting approach.

## Production Notes

Use sampled or performed emo/pop-punk guitar as the genre's defining melodic voice, since this direct cross-genre borrowing is what distinguishes emo rap from standard cloud rap or trap, and prioritize melodic, emotionally direct vocal delivery over rhythmic flow or dense lyrical content. Keep instrumental production sparse and atmospheric, and avoid treating the genre as generic trap with sad lyrics or over-correcting vocal delivery to the point of losing emotional authenticity — both miss what makes emo rap distinct.
