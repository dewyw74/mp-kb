## History

Blackgaze was pioneered by French musician Neige around 2005 through the projects Alcest and Amesoeurs, with Alcest's "Le Secret" EP (2005) described as the genre's birth, blending black metal's blast beats and shrieked vocals with the dreamy, reverb-soaked guitar textures of shoegaze bands like Cocteau Twins and My Bloody Valentine. Alcest's "Écailles de Lune" (2010) is widely regarded as one of the first fully realized blackgaze albums, and the genre rose to international prominence through San Francisco band Deafheaven, whose 2013 album "Sunbather" became one of blackgaze's most critically acclaimed and widely discussed releases, drawing new listeners to black metal's techniques while sparking debate among traditional black metal fans over the genre's crossover success.

## Music Theory

Blackgaze marries black metal's tremolo picking and blast beats with shoegaze's dreamy, wall-of-sound guitar texture and warmer harmonic color, frequently shifting toward relative major or brighter chord movement to produce a hopeful, bittersweet quality largely absent from traditional black metal's unrelenting minor-key hostility. Long, sustained chord washes built from multiple layered, chorused guitar tracks prioritize texture and emotional scale over the raw, thin riff definition of traditional black metal.

## Arrangement

Songs frequently open with clean, chorused or reverbed guitar establishing a dreamy mood before blast beats and tremolo picking enter, with extended, dynamics-driven builds — layering instrumental tracks and rising intensity over minutes — following post-rock and shoegaze song architecture rather than black metal's more static intensity. The genre's structural payoff is a climactic wall-of-sound peak rather than a conventional chorus hook, with quieter, cleaner passages (sometimes featuring sung rather than shrieked vocals) providing emotional contrast, directly inherited from shoegaze's soft/loud dynamic.

## Instruments

Layered, heavily chorused and reverbed tremolo-picked guitar creates the genre's defining dense, dreamy wall of sound, directly borrowing shoegaze production technique rather than black metal's traditionally thin, treble-focused necro tone. Blast beats and fast tremolo-driven drumming are retained from black metal but produced with considerably more clarity and dynamic sensitivity, supporting the genre's extended, dynamics-driven song builds.

## Sound Design

Lush, shoegaze-derived reverb and delay on layered guitar tracking builds the genre's signature wall-of-sound texture, requiring considerably higher-fidelity, more polished production than traditional lo-fi black metal, since blackgaze's beauty-focused aesthetic depends on clarity and lushness rather than raw murk. Chorus, flange, and reverb modulation on guitar are the genre's core texture-shaping devices, replacing black metal's raw, unmodulated tremolo tone.

## MIDI Programming

Program blast beats and tremolo-driven patterns with more dynamic sensitivity and clarity than lo-fi black metal drumming, and write tremolo-picked melodic lines that lean toward relative major or brighter harmonic color at key structural moments for the genre's characteristic bittersweet quality. Bass should carry more presence and fullness than in traditional black metal, supporting the genre's polished, expansive production.

## Mixing

The mix favors a fuller, warmer frequency balance than traditional black metal, with bass and low-mids present rather than gutted, and guitars layered and chorused rather than left thin. Extensive, lush reverb and modulated delay on guitar are genre-defining mix choices directly inherited from shoegaze production, with very wide stereo spread from layered guitar tracks creating the genre's signature immersive sound.

## Mastering

Masters target -11 to -9 LUFS integrated with a wider dynamic range than traditional black metal, preserving the audible contrast between quiet, dreamy passages and dense wall-of-sound peaks central to the genre's shoegaze-derived structure. Frequency balance is fuller and warmer than lo-fi black metal, with clear low-end presence and a lush, un-harsh high end.

## Reference Artists

Alcest's "Le Secret" (2005) is credited as the genre's birth, with "Écailles de Lune" (2010) regarded as one of the first fully realized blackgaze albums. Deafheaven's "Sunbather" (2013) is the genre's most widely recognized breakthrough recording, bringing blackgaze to a substantially wider audience.

## Production Notes

Layer multiple chorused/reverbed guitar takes of the same tremolo line to build the genre's signature dense, dreamy wall of sound, and use relative major shifts and warmer harmonic color at key structural moments to produce the genre's characteristic bittersweet, uplifting quality. Do not apply traditional black metal's raw, lo-fi necro production wholesale, since blackgaze depends on a fuller, more polished, reverb-lush sound, and do not confuse this genre with atmospheric black metal — atmospheric black metal draws its contrasting material from post-rock, folk, and ambient music while staying tonally somber and wintry, whereas blackgaze specifically borrows shoegaze's dreamier, brighter wall-of-sound guitar texture.
