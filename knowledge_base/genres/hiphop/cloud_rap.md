## History

Cloud rap emerged around 2010-2011 as artists like Lil B and producers like Clams Casino began pairing trap's 808-driven rhythmic foundation with dreamy, heavily reverbed synth textures borrowed from shoegaze and ambient electronic music, creating a hazier, more atmospheric alternative to mainstream trap's aggressive clarity. A$AP Rocky's early mixtapes brought the sound to a wider audience, and Swedish artist Yung Lean's internationally influential internet-native scene helped establish cloud rap as a genuinely global, internet-first subgenre rather than a single regional movement.

## Music Theory

Cloud rap harmony favors simple, often static or slowly moving vi-IV-I-V-style progressions (or minor-key i-VI-III-VII vamps) that function primarily as a stable harmonic canvas for extensive reverb and atmospheric processing rather than harmonic development in their own right. Melodic writing — whether sung, rapped, or auto-tuned somewhere between the two — favors long, sustained, reverb-friendly phrases over rhythmically dense delivery.

## Arrangement

Cloud rap structure follows a loose verse-hook form but de-emphasizes dynamic arrangement builds in favor of a consistently hazy, atmospheric mood sustained throughout — intros and outros frequently extend into pure ambient texture, dissolving rather than cutting cleanly in or out. Hooks are typically the most melodically (rather than rhythmically) developed sections, often sung and heavily harmonized.

## Instruments

Trap-derived 808 and hi-hat programming forms the rhythmic foundation but sits mixed further back and softer than in mainstream trap, allowing ethereal, heavily reverbed synth pads and plucks — the genre's defining textural signature — to dominate the sonic picture. Occasional shoegaze-influenced processed guitar textures reinforce the genre's dream-pop-adjacent atmospheric character.

## Sound Design

Extensive, often extreme reverb and delay are cloud rap's single most defining sound-design signature, treated as core compositional material rather than incidental mix polish. Auto-tune is frequently used expressively and atmospherically on vocals — shaping tone and texture as much as correcting pitch — reinforcing the genre's hazy, otherworldly vocal character.

## MIDI Programming

808 bass and hi-hat patterns stay simpler and less rhythmically aggressive than mainstream trap, supporting rather than driving the track's atmosphere. Melodic phrases favor long, sustained notes that reverb and delay can fully develop, rather than the rhythmically dense, rapid-fire patterns typical of more aggressive trap and drill subgenres.

## Mixing

Mixing keeps the drum foundation soft and understated, letting extensive reverb and wide, echoing delay on vocals and melodic elements create the genre's signature spacious, immersive atmosphere. The stereo image runs very wide and diffuse, directly reflecting the genre's shoegaze- and ambient-informed aesthetic roots.

## Mastering

Masters preserve moderate dynamics (typically -12 to -10 LUFS integrated) in service of the genre's floating, atmospheric character rather than pursuing maximum aggressive loudness, with an overall soft, hazy, reverb-heavy frequency balance.

## Reference Artists

Lil B's "I'm God" (produced by Clams Casino) is a foundational cloud rap text pairing atmospheric production with an idiosyncratic vocal delivery; A$AP Rocky's "Purple Swag" brought the sound's hazy, trap-adjacent atmosphere to a wider mainstream audience; Yung Lean's "Ghosttown" exemplifies the genre's melancholic, internet-native Scandinavian strand.

## Production Notes

Treat reverb and delay as genuinely compositional elements central to the track's identity, not just mixing polish applied at the end, and keep the 808/hi-hat foundation softer and further back than mainstream trap so atmosphere can lead. Use auto-tune expressively and atmospherically on vocals rather than purely as pitch correction, and avoid mixing drums too aggressively forward or under-using reverb/delay — both push the track back toward generic trap rather than cloud rap's genuinely hazy, floating identity.
