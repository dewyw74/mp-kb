## History

"Afrobeats" (with an 's') is a modern, global pop phenomenon that emerged from West Africa (primarily Nigeria and Ghana) in the early 2000s. It should not be confused with "Afrobeat" (no 's'), the highly political, 10-minute long, jazz/funk fusion genre pioneered by Nigerian legend Fela Kuti in the 1970s. Modern Afrobeats takes the complex, syncopated rhythms of traditional African music and Fela Kuti's Highlife guitars, and fuses them with the tight, 3-minute pop structures, Auto-Tuned vocals, and heavy 808s of American Hip Hop, R&B, and Dancehall. Following massive crossover hits like Drake's "One Dance" and Wizkid's "Essence," Afrobeats has become one of the most dominant and influential dance genres in the world.

## Music Theory

Harmonically, Afrobeats leans heavily into the smooth, minor-key chord progressions of R&B and Neo-Soul (e.g., i - v - VI - VII). The complexity of the genre lies entirely in its rhythm. It relies heavily on the "clave" and the "tresillo" rhythm—a 3:2 polyrhythmic structure where syncopated accents (often played on a rimshot or conga) dance around the steady 4/4 pulse of the kick drum.

## Arrangement

Arrangements follow modern, radio-friendly pop structures. The goal is to establish an irresistible, hypnotic groove immediately in the intro and maintain it throughout the track. Arrangement movement is achieved not by changing chords, but by a process of addition and subtraction: dropping the kick drum out for the verse, bringing in a shaker for the pre-chorus, and adding a lush synth pad or vocal harmony for the chorus.

## Instruments

The rhythm section is a hybrid: heavy, electronic kick drums and deep sub-basses paired with organic, acoustic percussion (shakers, congas, talking drums). Melodic instruments usually include muted, rhythmic electric guitars (playing in the "Highlife" picking style), electric pianos (Rhodes), and warm, plucky synthesizers (marimbas/mallets).

## Sound Design

The sound palette is warm, tropical, and smooth. Harsh, distorted sounds (like heavy rock guitars or aggressive dubstep synths) are generally avoided. The 808 sub-basses are EQ'd to be round and deep rather than distorted. Vocal production utilizes Auto-Tune, but often in a smoother, more R&B-oriented way than the robotic use found in modern Rap.

## MIDI Programming

The secret to Afrobeats MIDI programming is "Swing." The core kick drum may be quantized to the grid, but the hi-hats, shakers, and percussion must be swung (shifted slightly off the grid) to create the relaxed, human "pocket" that makes the genre danceable. Basslines are highly syncopated, often avoiding the downbeat entirely to emphasize the 'and' of the beat.

## Mixing

An Afrobeats mix is warm and vocal-centric. The low-end must be massive and punchy for club playback, but it must be tightly controlled so it doesn't swallow the complex percussion. The acoustic percussion elements (shakers, congas) are panned wide and EQ'd to sound crisp and organic. The lead vocal is mixed front-and-center, often utilizing slapback delays rather than long reverbs to keep the rhythmic phrasing distinct.

## Mastering

Mastered loud for modern streaming and club play (-8 to -10 LUFS). The master must retain the dynamic "snap" of the rimshots and hand drums. Over-limiting will crush the transients of the percussion, destroying the syncopated bounce that defines the genre.

## Reference Artists

Wizkid and Davido were crucial in breaking the genre globally in the 2010s. Burna Boy introduced a heavier, more Dancehall-influenced "Afro-fusion" sound. Rema, Asake, and Tems represent the current, globally dominant wave of the genre, blending it seamlessly with American R&B and South African Amapiano.

## Production Notes

To produce authentic Afrobeats, you must master the polyrhythmic "bounce." Start with a four-on-the-floor kick, but place your snare or rimshot on syncopated off-beats (the 3+3+2 tresillo rhythm). Add a shaker loop and apply heavy swing. For harmony, use lush, jazzy minor chords on a Rhodes piano or a warm synth pad. Finally, add a highly rhythmic, muted electric guitar line playing single notes. Ensure the bassline interlocks with the kick drum, and leave plenty of space in the midrange for the vocal.
