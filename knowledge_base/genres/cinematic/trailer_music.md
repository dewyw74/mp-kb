## History

Trailer Music (or "Epic Music") is a specialized offshoot of cinematic film scoring designed specifically for movie and video game marketing campaigns. In the early 2000s, production music libraries (like Two Steps From Hell and Audiomachine) began composing standalone, highly aggressive, 2-to-3 minute orchestral tracks because actual film scores were rarely finished in time to be used in the trailers. The genre shifted dramatically in 2010 with the trailer for *Inception* (featuring music by Zack Hemsey), which introduced the massive, distorted, synth/brass hybrid sound known as the "Braam." Today, trailer music blends massive orchestral sampling with extreme electronic sound design and EDM mixing techniques.

## Music Theory

Trailer music relies on the simplest, most emotionally immediate harmonic progressions available. It almost exclusively uses minor keys (usually D minor or C minor to maximize the lowest, most aggressive notes of the cello and bass sections). The "Epic" progression (i - VI - III - VII) is ubiquitous. Melodies are short, repetitive, and designed to be instantly memorable within a 15-second TV spot.

## Arrangement

The structure is the most rigid of any musical genre. It must follow the 3-Act trailer format:
1. **Act I**: Quiet, atmospheric, tense (0:00 - 0:45).
2. **Act II**: The build. Rhythmic strings and pulsing synths enter (0:45 - 1:30).
3. **Act III**: The climax. The "Backend." Maximum volume, massive choirs, and soaring brass (1:30 - 2:15).
These sections *must* be separated by sudden, dramatic pauses ("stop-downs") and massive impacts ("hits" or "whoosh-bangs") to allow video editors to place text or dialogue.

## Instruments

The instrumentation is a hybrid of a massive, aggressive symphony orchestra and a modern electronic sound-design lab. The acoustic percussion (Taiko drums, anvils, toms) is massive. The brass section (specifically the low horns and trombones) is pushed to play as aggressively and loudly as possible. Synthesizers provide pulsing rhythms and earth-shaking sub-bass.

## Sound Design

Sound design is the core of the genre. Traditional acoustic instruments are processed to sound terrifying and modern. The "Braam" (a distorted, pitch-dropped horn/synth hybrid), the "Riser" (a long, ascending synthetic sound used to build tension before a hit), and the "Suck" (a reversed cymbal or impact) are the fundamental tools of the trade. 

## MIDI Programming

Programming requires top-tier, modern orchestral sample libraries (e.g., Spitfire Audio, Cinematic Studio, Heavyocity). The programming must be heavily quantized and velocity-maxed in Act III. String "spiccato" patches (very short, aggressive bowing) are used to create the relentless, driving 16th-note motor of the track.

## Mixing

Mix a trailer track exactly like a modern Dubstep or Hard Rock track. Do not respect the natural acoustics of the orchestra. Heavily compress the brass to make it tear through the speakers. EQ aggressive "click" into the percussion. Use sidechain compression to duck the massive reverb tails out of the way of the fast string arpeggios. 

## Mastering

Trailer music is mastered to the absolute extreme limits of digital loudness (-6 to -8 LUFS), competing directly with Top 40 pop and EDM. The master must sound incredibly wide, bright, and feature terrifying, theater-shaking sub-bass.

## Reference Artists

Two Steps From Hell popularized the massive, melodic, "epic choir" orchestral sound. Audiomachine provides the backbone of modern Hollywood marketing. Zack Hemsey defined the modern, slow-burn, synth-heavy "Inception" sound. Hidden Citizens specialize in the hugely popular "Epic Pop Cover" format.

## Production Notes

To write successful trailer music, you must think like a video editor. Your music exists solely to make 2 seconds of video footage look terrifying or incredibly exciting. Build in edit points. Use a "Riser" for 4 bars, cut the entire track to dead silence for 1 beat, and then hit the listener with the loudest, most distorted orchestral/synth impact possible. The dynamic shock between absolute silence and absolute volume is the secret to the genre.
