## History

Hyperpop grew out of 2010s bubblegum bass and the UK's PC Music label (SOPHIE, A. G. Cook), which pushed pop production conventions to exaggerated, hyper-glossy extremes as a form of pop art commentary. The style consolidated into a named, self-aware genre around 2019, catalyzed by Spotify's "hyperpop" playlist and breakout acts like 100 gecs, who fused emo/pop-punk sensibility with EDM-scale maximalism and internet-native genre-collision. Charli XCX's collaborations with SOPHIE and PC Music producers form a direct bridge from mainstream electropop into the genre's more extreme aesthetic.

## Music Theory

Harmony frequently takes a back seat to texture and pitch manipulation — many hyperpop tracks ride simple, repetitive pop progressions (or a static vamp) while extreme pitch-shifting and effect processing substitute for conventional harmonic or melodic development. Where hooks exist, they are pushed to a deliberately exaggerated, maximalist, almost cartoonish degree, treating pop songcraft conventions as material to distort rather than follow.

## Arrangement

Hyperpop rejects mainstream pop's gradual, telegraphed build-and-release pacing in favor of abrupt full-intensity entries, compressed and exaggerated EDM-style builds, and maximalist drops layering pitched vocals, distorted bass, and bitcrushed synths. Bridges are often the most experimental section of the song, featuring tempo/key shifts or an abrupt genre pivot, and outros frequently end on a hard cut rather than a conventional fade.

## Instruments

Distorted, often clipped 808s and hyper-processed drum machine hits anchor the rhythm section, paired with heavily saturated sub bass or aggressive future-bass-style supersaw bass. Bright, intentionally 'ugly' bitcrushed or digital synths and kawaii-bass/future-bass plucks provide the genre's textural signature, with occasional distorted pop-punk/emo guitar samples used for deliberate genre-blending.

## Sound Design

Wavetable and FM synthesis generate bright, aggressive leads, while extreme pitch-shifting (including chipmunk/nightcore-style vocal processing) functions as a primary compositional device rather than a novelty effect. Heavy bitcrush, distortion, and glitch/stutter editing are applied liberally and audibly across the mix as genre-defining textures, not flaws to be minimized.

## MIDI Programming

Drum and bass programming borrows aggressively from trap and future bass, pushed to distorted extremes, with dense, glitchy patterns and frequent stutter-edits. Melodic hooks are frequently doubled or tripled with pitch-shifted vocal layers for a hyper-bright, exaggerated effect, and humanization is largely rejected in favor of obviously synthetic, robotic processing as a deliberate aesthetic statement.

## Mixing

Mixing embraces aggressive brightness and intentional harshness in the high end, paired with a hard, distorted low end, using extremely heavy, often clipping-adjacent compression and limiting for maximal, in-your-face loudness. Stereo imaging is extremely wide and often deliberately disorienting, and heavy, fast automation on filters, pitch, and effect sends sustains the genre's constantly shifting, maximalist energy.

## Mastering

Masters are pushed extremely loud, often -7 to -5 LUFS integrated or louder, deliberately embracing audible distortion or clipping as part of the genre's maximalist identity rather than avoiding it as a mastering flaw.

## Reference Artists

SOPHIE's "Bipp" and the broader PC Music catalog established hyperpop's hyper-glossy, exaggerated aesthetic; 100 gecs' "money machine" catalyzed the genre's mainstream breakout and named the style; Charli XCX's "Vroom Vroom" bridges mainstream electropop and hyperpop's more extreme production choices; Dorian Electra and Rico Nasty extend the genre's maximalist, genre-collision approach.

## Production Notes

Treat distortion, clipping, and pitch manipulation as primary compositional tools rather than mix problems requiring correction, and let genre references collide abruptly and freely — genre-collision is a core aesthetic device in hyperpop, not a mistake. Avoid cleaning up intentionally harsh elements or applying conventional pop dynamic restraint, both of which strip away the genre's defining maximalist identity; treat pitch-shifted vocals as a genuine compositional and emotional device rather than a gimmick.
